# Indian History Notes for RAG

## Ancient India

### Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE)

The Indus Valley Civilization was one of the earliest urban civilizations in the world. Important cities included Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, Dholavira, and Lothal. The civilization was known for advanced town planning, drainage systems, standardized weights, and trade with Mesopotamia. The Great Bath at Mohenjo-Daro suggests ritual practices. The script of the civilization remains undeciphered. The civilization declined due to climate change, river drying, and migration.

---

### Vedic Period (1500–500 BCE)

The Vedic Period followed the decline of the Indus Valley Civilization. During this period, the four Vedas — Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda — were composed. Sanskrit became an important language. Society developed the varna system, and religious life focused on yajnas and worship of gods like Indra, Agni, and Varuna. The foundations of the Mahabharata and Ramayana also emerged during this time.

---

### Buddhism and Jainism

Buddhism was founded by Gautama Buddha, who attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya and gave his first sermon at Sarnath. Buddhism emphasized the Four Noble Truths, the Eightfold Path, and non-violence. Jainism was founded by Mahavira and focused on strict non-violence, truth, and non-attachment. Both religions challenged ritualism and caste hierarchy.

---

### Maurya Empire

The Maurya Empire was founded by Chandragupta Maurya with the guidance of Chanakya, the author of Arthashastra. Ashoka became the greatest Mauryan ruler after the Kalinga War transformed him spiritually. He adopted Buddhism and promoted dhamma, non-violence, and welfare policies. Ashoka spread Buddhism across Asia through missionaries and rock edicts.

---

### Gupta Empire (Golden Age)

The Gupta Empire is known as the Golden Age of India because of achievements in science, mathematics, literature, and art. Samudragupta expanded the empire, while Chandragupta II supported scholars like Kalidasa. Aryabhata contributed to mathematics and astronomy, including concepts related to zero and planetary motion. Nalanda became an important center of learning during this period.

---

# Medieval India

## Delhi Sultanate

The Delhi Sultanate was ruled by the Slave, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, and Lodi dynasties. Alauddin Khilji strengthened the empire through military campaigns and market reforms. Muhammad bin Tughlaq introduced experimental policies like token currency and shifting the capital. Indo-Islamic architecture and the Urdu language developed during this period.

---

## Vijayanagara Empire

The Vijayanagara Empire was founded in South India and became a major center of culture and trade. Krishnadevaraya was its greatest ruler and supported literature, temple architecture, and administration. The capital city Hampi became famous for its prosperity and monuments.

---

## Mughal Empire

The Mughal Empire was founded by Babur after the First Battle of Panipat in 1526. Akbar expanded the empire and promoted religious tolerance through policies like Sulh-i-Kul. Shah Jahan built the Taj Mahal, one of the greatest examples of Mughal architecture. Aurangzeb expanded the empire further but faced resistance from regional powers like the Marathas.

---

## Maratha Empire

The Maratha Empire was founded by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who used guerrilla warfare and built a strong administration and navy. Under the Peshwas, especially Bajirao I, the Maratha Empire expanded across much of India. The Third Battle of Panipat weakened Maratha power significantly.

---

# Colonial India

## British Expansion in India

The British East India Company gradually expanded its power after the Battle of Plassey in 1757 and the Battle of Buxar in 1764. British policies transformed them from traders into rulers. Through wars and policies like the Doctrine of Lapse, the British gained control over most of India.

---

## Revolt of 1857

The Revolt of 1857 was the first large-scale uprising against British rule. It began at Meerut and spread across northern India. Leaders like Rani Lakshmibai, Nana Sahib, Tantia Tope, and Bahadur Shah Zafar played important roles. The revolt failed, but it ended East India Company rule and brought India under direct British Crown control.

---

# Indian Freedom Struggle

## Rise of Nationalism

The Indian National Congress was founded in 1885 and became the leading organization in the freedom movement. Leaders like Dadabhai Naoroji, Bal Gangadhar Tilak, and Gopal Krishna Gokhale promoted political awareness and self-rule.

---

## Mahatma Gandhi and Mass Movements

Mahatma Gandhi transformed the freedom struggle into a mass movement through non-violence and satyagraha. Important movements included the Non-Cooperation Movement, Civil Disobedience Movement, Dandi March, and Quit India Movement. Gandhi emphasized ahimsa and peaceful resistance against British rule.

---

## Revolutionary Leaders

Bhagat Singh became a symbol of revolutionary nationalism through his actions against British officials and institutions. Subhas Chandra Bose formed the Indian National Army to fight British rule with foreign support. Chandrashekhar Azad also played a major role in revolutionary activities.

---

## Independence and Partition (1947)

India became independent on 15 August 1947 after the partition of British India into India and Pakistan. Partition caused large-scale migration and communal violence. Jawaharlal Nehru delivered the famous “Tryst with Destiny” speech at independence. Mahatma Gandhi was assassinated in 1948.

---

# Modern India

## Constitution of India

India became a republic on 26 January 1950 when the Constitution came into force. Dr. B. R. Ambedkar chaired the drafting committee. The Constitution established democracy, federalism, secularism, and Fundamental Rights.

---

## Economic Reforms (1991)

India introduced economic liberalization in 1991 under Prime Minister P. V. Narasimha Rao and Finance Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh. The reforms promoted privatization, globalization, and foreign investment, leading to rapid economic growth.

---

## ISRO and Space Achievements

India made major achievements in space research through ISRO. Important missions include Aryabhata, Chandrayaan-1, Mangalyaan, and Chandrayaan-3. India became the first country to land near the lunar south pole with Chandrayaan-3.
